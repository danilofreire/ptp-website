Political Theory Project

“Ordeals, Sassywood and Trial by Battle: Legal Processes and Punishments Unlike our Own”

  • Lectures
button
Mar 19
4:00pm - 6:00pm
Barus & Holley, Room 159

Ever wonder how legal conflicts and criminal trials operated before modern times? Was the world as cutthroat and chaotic under the ancient regime as commonly thought? Or, could apparently violent and seemingly irrational social customs such as ordeals, consumption of poisonous tree bark, and trials by battle, have served critical functions in promoting social order within their respective times and places?

Peter Leeson, The Duncan Black Professor of Economics and Law at George Mason University, has produced a vibrant research stream dedicated to such questions and will be visiting Brown University on Thursday March 19 to share his thoughts and present such work. Please join us in Barus and Holley Room 159 at 4:00 p.m.
