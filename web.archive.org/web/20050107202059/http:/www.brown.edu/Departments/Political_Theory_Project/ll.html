he Political Theory Project sponsors a weekly brown bag luncheon series throughout the year. All undergraduates are welcome to attend these Liberty Lunches. Liberty Lunches provide a place for students to meet informally with the Visiting Fellows in the Political Theory Project, and to talk with them about economic and philosophical aspects of liberty. Professor John Tomasi will chair these Lunches During the 2004-5 year, Liberty Lunches will be held on Tuesdays in the Seminar Room of Blistein House, between 1.00 and 3.00. Short readings for each week (typically 3-10 pages) can be picked up before the Lunches in the offices of the Political Theory Project, which are located on the third floor of Blistein House. The reading schedule is below. We hope you will find the readings fun, and provocative. For each Liberty Lunch, the Political Theory Project will provide soft drinks. On special occassions, we will also supply plates of Freedom Fries. All are welcome!
