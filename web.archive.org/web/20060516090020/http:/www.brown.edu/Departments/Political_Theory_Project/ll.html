The Political Theory Project sponsors a bi-weekly luncheon series throughout the year at Brown's prestigious Faculty Club. Liberty Lunches provide a place for undergraduates interested in the opportunities and challenges faced by free societies to meet informally with the Visiting Fellows in the Political Theory Project to discuss selected topics. This year, the luncheons will be chaired by Professor John Tomasi and will feature "the Moral Foundations of Capitalism" as a guiding theme.

Liberty Lunches will usually be held on Tuesdays between 12:00 and 12:50 and will extend into the afternoon for those interested. To stimulate conversation, each week students will be expected to be familiar with short readings (typically 5-15 pages), which just goes to show that there really is no such thing as a free lunch...

Participation each week is open to all. Priority is given to students who register in advance however, as this helps us plan the events more effectively. Please do not sign up for weeks that you cannot attend. Registration is open two weeks prior to any event. Once registered, participants will receive an email confirmation. Register now by sending an email to John_Phillips@brown.edu with your name, e-mail, and year of graduation.
