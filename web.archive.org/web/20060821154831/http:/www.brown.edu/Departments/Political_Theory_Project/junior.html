The Brown Political Theory Project is happy to announce the creation of its Junior Fellows Forum. The purpose of the Forum is to identify and encourage Brown undergraduates who are interested in the principles that underlie a society of free, prosperous and equal persons. The Forum provides opportunities for select Brown undergraduates to interact with Brown faculty, Visiting Fellows in the Political Theory Project, and to engage in sustained discussion with Brown undergraduates holding diverse opinions on the political issues of our day.

     Membership in the Junior Fellows Forum includes the following benefits:

  • Being named a Junior Fellow in Brown's Political Theory Project (PTP).


  • Name and year of graduation listed on the Political Theory Project's home-page.


  • Opportunities to enjoy PTP sponsored lunches at Brown's Faculty Club.


  • Invitations to special seminars on political and economic theory, conducted exclusively for the members of the PTP's Junior Fellows Forum.


  • Plan's are underway for an expense-paid weekend retreat for PTP Junior Fellows during a weekend of the Spring semester 2005-6.


  • Guidance and contacts for summer internships in Washington DC, for week-long summer seminars held at universities around the country, and about special tuition fellowships available to Brown students.
     If you would like to be considered for membership in the Junior Fellows Forum of the Political Theory Project, submit by email a letter of application indicating your name, email address, concentration, and anticipated graduation date from Brown to: John_Phillips@brown.edu. Please attach an essay of no more than 300 words outlining your interest in political thought and your conception of the principles and institutions that underlie a society of free, prosperous, and equal persons.

      Applications to the Junior Fellows Forum will be considered on a rolling-admissions basis, with deadlines occurring on the 15th day of each month. Criteria for admission are limited strictly to the depth and seriousness of each student's interest in political ideas, completely regardless of the orientation of those ideas. Only undergraduate students at Brown University are eligible to apply.
