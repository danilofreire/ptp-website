An itemization of the Political Theory Project's major needs for the 2005-6 academic follows:

Liberty Lunches: 20 Lunches at $100 apiece. Each Monday during term time, the Project hosts a brown-bag lunch at which Brown undergraduates meet with Project Fellows to discuss an important theme about liberty. Your donation pays for reading materials and soft drinks for participants.

Public Lectures: 4 Lectures at $2,500-$10,000 apiece. The Political Theory Project hosts public lectures on campus, with a special interest speakers who thoughtfully challenge prevailing moral and political orthodoxies. Your donation brings such a speaker to Brown by paying speaking fees and travel expenses, plus advertising costs.

Free Your Mind Conference: 1 Conference at $35,000. Early each fall term, the Project hosts a weekend-long conference to introduce a large group of Brown Undergraduates, and especially members of the incoming Freshmen class, to the idea of a free society. Your donation allows us to bring in exciting speakers from around the country, to provide reading materials and t-shirts for students, meals, and to cover our advertising and programming expenses.

Political Theory Project Visiting Fellowships: 5 Fellowships at $52,500 apiece. The Visiting Fellows program is the heart of the Political Theory Project, and its most important feature by far. Your donation allows the Project to select a young scholar to spend a crucial year at Brown, early in her or his career. The fellow conducts original research, interacts regularly with Brown faculty and graduate and undergraduate students, and, most important, adds exciting new Political Theory Project sponsored courses to the Brown undergraduate curriculum.

Naming rights for each of these events, and for Brown's Political Theory Project as a whole, are negotiable with the Project director and with Brown's administration and Development Office.
